General Booking Tips

I've flown to and from Japan a couple of times, while I'm no expert, I've learned a couple of tricks for booking.

  1. 1. Book well in advance

    Flights to Japan are usually at their cheapest 5-6 months prior to departure.

  2. 2. Track prices

    It's a good idea to check every couple of days to see if prices have dropped. Alternatively, you can track prices using Google Flights. They will send you an email any time the price fluctuates, up or down.

  3. 3. Book with an aggregator

    This should be no shock to anyone. Online search engines are nearly always cheaper than buying directly from the carrier. My recommendation is the aforementioned Google Flights, though I hear good things about Priceline and Expedia.

  4. 4. Fly to Narita

    Far and away Japan's cheapest arrival airport is Narita International Airport (NRT). It is located just east of Tokyo, so you will need to take a train or bus into Tokyo proper. Even including the cost of additional transportation it is still cheaper than (the more downtown) Haneda International Airport (HND). Both are viable options, but you will save around $200 going to Narita.
